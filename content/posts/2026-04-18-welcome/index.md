## Why This Exists

AI is moving fast. That's not an observation — it's an understatement. In any given week, there are new model releases, new benchmark records, new research directions, new tools, and new arguments about what any of it means. Following the field seriously is a full-time job.

Most students don't have that. They have coursework, projects, part-time jobs, and the rest of life. The result is that most CS and AI students at university are six months to two years behind what's actually happening in the field. By the time a development makes it into a textbook or a module syllabus, it's already been superseded.

AIDA Daily Digest is our answer to that problem.

## What We Cover

Every day, we publish one focused piece on the most important AI/ML development of the past 24 hours. It could be:

- A research paper that changes how we think about a problem
- A model release worth understanding (not just knowing about)
- A tool or library that shifts what's practical to build
- A shift in the field — companies, infrastructure, policy — that has real technical implications

The selection criterion is simple: *would knowing this make you a better engineer or researcher next month?* If yes, it's in. If it's noise, it isn't.

## What We Don't Cover

We don't cover hype. We don't republish press releases with a thin layer of paraphrase on top. We don't write "X just changed everything" posts unless we can explain precisely what changed and precisely why it matters.

We also don't write for a general audience. The digest is for students who are already building — people who understand what a transformer is, who know what fine-tuning means, who are trying to close the gap between what they learned in class and what the field is actually doing.

If you want surface-level AI news, there are dozens of newsletters for that. This isn't one of them.

## The Format

**Daily digest.** Published each day, usually in the early afternoon. One main topic, explained properly. Context, technical detail where it matters, and an honest assessment of significance. Typically 400–700 words.

**Sunday weekly recap.** Every Sunday, we zoom out. A curated summary of the week's main themes — what mattered, what the threads were, and what to watch in the coming week. This is where we connect the dots.

Posts are written by AIDA members. We have a workflow and editorial process — pieces go through at least one review before publication. The goal is quality, not volume. We'd rather publish nothing on a slow news day than publish something that wastes your time.

## Who It's For

Students in CS, AI, data science, and adjacent fields who are serious about the discipline. Primarily Ajman University students, but the digest is public — if you've found it and it's useful, it's for you.

You don't need to be an expert to read it. You do need to be willing to think.

## How to Follow Along

The best way is RSS. Add `/index.xml` to your feed reader and you'll get every post as it goes live. No algorithm, no feed ranking, no noise.
