## What We Are

AIDA — the AI & Data Science Club at Ajman University — exists because the gap between university coursework and what's actually happening in AI is enormous, and it's widening every month. We're a student-run club built to close that gap.

We are not a society. We don't throw events for the sake of attendance numbers. AIDA is a working group: students who take AI/ML seriously and want to build real technical depth alongside people who share that drive.

## What We Do

**Daily Digests.** Every day we publish a short, honest breakdown of the most important AI/ML development of the past 24 hours — a paper, a model release, a tool, a shift in the field. No hype, no regurgitated press releases. We explain what it is, why it matters, and what it means for people building in this space.

**Weekly Recaps.** Every Sunday we zoom out: a curated summary of the week's themes, with context that the daily format doesn't allow.

**Weekly Meetings.** We meet once a week to go deeper — paper reading sessions, live coding, project reviews, and guest talks from people actually working in AI. Meetings are focused. If you show up, expect to contribute.

**Project Sprints.** Every few months we run a focused sprint around a real problem: building a model, contributing to open source, or producing a piece of research worth sharing. These aren't hackathons with participation certificates. They're an attempt to make something real.

## Who We Are For

Primarily Ajman University students in CS, AI, data science, and related programmes. But we care more about motivation than major — if you want to build technical depth in AI/ML and you're willing to do the work, AIDA is for you.

## Where We're Going

The digest is the foundation. From it, we're building a community with enough collective knowledge to tackle serious problems. The goal isn't to look good on a CV — it's to actually understand what's happening in this field, and eventually shape it.

If that sounds like where you want to be, [join us](/join/).
